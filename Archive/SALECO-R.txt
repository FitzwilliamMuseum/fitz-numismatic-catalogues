ROLLIN, C., Paris;  London

Rollin & Feuardent claimed to be the world's oldest proper coin
dealership.  Founded by Charles-Louis Rollin (1777-1853) who
began his career as a money-changer in the galleries of the
Palais Royal, published his first coin sales list in 1811, and
moved in the 1840's to premises at 12 rue Vivienne opposite the
Bibliothèque Royale.  The business passed to the third generation
in the hands of Felix Feuardent (1819-1907), a provincial
librarian who became a partner in 1859, and in 1860 married the 
daughter of Charles-Louis' son,  Camile.  As scholars, dealers and
auctioneers they became pre-eminent in fin de siècle Paris, running a
branch in London as well. [Italo Vecchi]
