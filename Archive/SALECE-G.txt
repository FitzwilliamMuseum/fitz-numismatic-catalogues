GAETTENS, R.A., Heidelberg;  from 1959 or 1960, Lübeck, Germany
  -- son of the scholar R. Gaettens (sen.) who was in business
     partnership with the dealer RIECHMANN

     * Riechmann disappears after his last auction in 1934.
Richard Gaettens (sen.), his associate in A. Riechmann & Co.,
survived the war and moved to Heidelberg where his son, Richard
GAETTENS jun., began his own numismatic business. His auction
catalogues are labelled as of "Richard Gaettens" (4-5, 7, 11) or
"Richard Gaettens jun." (1-3, 6, 8-10, 12-13). Whether this is
just editorial variety, or an indication of the father sharing
the business with the son, is not known.  (The son's later
auctions and lists issued in Lübeck are all under the simple name
"Richard Gaettens".)
     What purports to be the final auction of A. RIECHMANN & Co.
(q.v.) was conducted in Heidelberg in 1959 by "Richard Gaettens",
whether father or son, perhaps as an act of homage to the
original firm, which had actually closed twenty-five years
earlier.  The contents were literary -- the library of the
Sammlung Julius, whose coins and medals were sold under the name
of GAETTENS.
     Gaettens jr. continued to use the name of A. Riechmann & Co.
in Lübeck on his own lit. lists.

====================

   NB:  Beginning early on, the prices realized at auction were
reported by reprinting the auction catalogues (some? all?) after the sale
with the prices ealized inserted as marginalia.  In the later years of this 
system an illustrated catalogue was often reprinted unillustrated.


     (oi) original llustrated
     (ou) original unillustrated
     (ri) reprint (priced), illustrated
     (ru) reprint (priced), unillustrated
     (oi pr), (ou pr) -- prices are hand-written in the original
             catalogue.

       In this library, when both the original and the prices-
realized reprint are illustrated (oi, ri), or both are
unillustrated (ou, ru), we have usually endeavoured to acquire
only the reprint (i.e. including both the prices realized and the
illustrations where produced).  When the original was illustrated
(oi), but not the reprint with prices realized (ru), we aim to
acquire both.

       Reprinting of the catalogues ceased after 1973 when the
customary independent Prices Realized List was introduced.
